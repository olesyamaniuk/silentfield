FAQ

  • Is Gem Forest free to play?
    Yes, you can play for free and progress through the islands without paying. The game may offer optional purchases for boosters or convenience, but they’re not required to enjoy the core adventure. If you like to take it slow and play smart, you’ll still go far.
  • Do I need an internet connection?
    Not always. Gem Forest supports offline play, so you can enjoy levels anywhere — commute, flight mode, quiet evenings. Some event features or updates may require a connection, but the main puzzle journey is designed to stay playable without Wi-Fi.
  • How do I create powerful gems and combos?
    Match 4 or 5 gems to form special pieces that clear more space. The real magic happens when you combine special gems together — that’s where chain reactions and big clears come from. If a level feels tight, try planning one move ahead to set up a combo instead of clearing immediately.
  • Are there time limits on levels?
    No. We made this game for people who want to chill out, so there's no rush - take your time. That means you can experiment, learn patterns, and enjoy the puzzle without pressure. It’s strategy-first, not speed-first.
  • What are “missions” and why do they matter?
    Missions are the goals that guide your progress through the forest. Completing them unlocks new islands and keeps the adventure moving forward. They also help the levels feel varied, so you’re not just clearing gems — you’re advancing a journey.
  • Will the game get harder?
    Yes, gradually — and that’s part of the satisfaction. Early levels teach the rhythm, then the forest starts asking for cleaner planning and smarter booster use. Sure, it gets harder, but in a good way. You can actually tell you're getting better at it instead of just hitting a wall.
  • How do daily rewards and events work?
    Daily rewards give you small boosts for simply returning — gems, bonuses, and helpful items. Events appear as limited-time challenges with special rewards, giving you something fresh without forcing you to abandon your main path. You can participate when it feels fun, and ignore it when you just want calm levels.
